2M Parameter Byte-Level GPT Optimization

Objective

Optimize a small GPT language model under strict constraints: maximum 2000 optimizer steps, maximum 2 million parameters, CPU-only training, and training only on the provided corpus.

Final Architecture

  • Byte-level tokenizer (vocab size: 256)
  • Transformer decoder architecture
  • SwiGLU feed-forward layers
  • Weight tying between embedding and output projection
  • AdamW optimizer with warmup and cosine learning rate schedule
  • Gradient clipping

Why This Model Performs Better

The main improvement came from replacing the standard MLP block with SwiGLU. The gated activation provides better feature learning while staying within the parameter budget. Weight tying allowed more capacity to be allocated elsewhere in the network. Training changes such as AdamW, learning-rate scheduling, and removing excessive regularization improved optimization under the limited 2000-step budget.

Human and AI Contribution

AI tools were used for brainstorming possible improvements, debugging errors, and explaining architecture choices. The final experiment selection, parameter decisions, implementation, training runs, and evaluation comparisons were performed manually.
